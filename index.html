Squirrel

Redesigning the on-boarding and set-up of a FinTech startup.

  • Client: Squirrel - Date: June 2017 - Service: UX Design.

Summary

Squirrel is a bank account, controlled by an app, aimed at making managing your money easy. We completely redesigned the onboarding, signup, and activation process for their app.

Problem

A number of users had downloaded the app which had a convoluted onboarding and setup process. Users were skipping through this onboarding and consequently, on first using the app, didn't really understand how to use it. The result of this was they never continued using Squirrel.

Solution

We took the 3 stage process of onboarding, signup and activation, merging all three into one combined linear flow, cutting the number of steps, and vastly shortening the copy by augmenting it with icons and diagrams to reduce cognitive load of a previously text heavy experience. We were also able to gain the user's trust in using the app and the service by highlighting early on the types of protection their money had by using the familiar logos of the bank that was protecting their money, and the financial standards agency that authorised and regulated Squirrel.

Tools and Techniques

Competitor Analysis, Surveys, User Interviews, Personas, User Journeys and Experience Maps, Design Studio, Design Workshops, Paper Prototyping, Wireframing in Sketch, InVision Prototypes, User Testing, Design Specifications.

AirFrance

Enhancing the in-flight services experience through an app

  • Client: AirFrance/General Assembly - Date: April 2017 - Service: UX Design

Summary

Air France is the French flagship carrier. We were tasked with integrating Air France’s in-flight services into a mobile first, responsive website or a native mobile or tablet application.

Problem

The current AirFrance app was very limited, offering ticket booking and flight tracking and that was pretty much it. The experience of almost every in-flight service could potentially be improved by somehow integrating it into the mobile App so we investigated the services that were currently causing the biggest pain points for both the flyers and the flight attendants. These included the time taken ordering Snacks & Drinks, browsing Entertainment on unresponsive in-seat touch screens and ordering items from the duty-free catalogue.

Solution

We redesigned the current AirFrance app to integrate dedicated in-flight services features including entertainment, food and drink, duty-free shopping, and purchasing wifi.

Tools and Techniques

Feature Comparisons, Surveys, User Interviews, Personas and User Journeys, MoSCow Method, Design Workshops, Paper Prototyping, Wireframing in Sketch, Marvel and InVision Prototypes, User Testing, Design Specifications.

Growing Garden Supplies

A gardening supplies shopping website.

  • Date: March 2017
  • Client: General Assembly
  • Service: UX Design

Introduction

Growing Garden Supplies is a small, independent Garden Supplies store based in East London since 1982. It is hypothetical but it is based closely on a real garden supply center in the same locality.

The Brief

To create a desktop first, interactive prototype for a website for Growing Garden Supplies - a small, independent Garden Supplies store based in East London since 1982. To focus on designing the experience of shopping online. Match the user personas, business goals and brand values of the company.

Tools and Techniques

Competitor Analysis (including Feature Comparisons and Heuristic Comparisons), User Interviews, Concept Mapping, Personas, User Journeys, Feature Prioritisation, Information Architecture (Open & Closed Card Sorting), Design Workshops, Sketching, Paper Prototyping, Wireframing in Omnigraffle, Marvel and InVision Prototypes, User Testing.
